## Executive Summary

- **Ritual events:** 50.7% proximity to policy events (±14 days)
- **Holiday baseline:** 19.9% proximity
- **Ratio:** 3.5x above baseline
- **Statistical significance:** p = 0.002 (Mann-Whitney U test)

### Statistical Tests

1. **Proximity Analysis:** Count policy events within ±14 days of ritual vs. holiday markers
2. **Mann-Whitney U Test:** Non-parametric comparison of proximity distributions
3. **Permutation Testing:** Randomization to verify significance isn't artifact
4. **Lag Analysis:** Measure mean temporal distance from markers

### Control Design

Annual holidays (Passover, Rosh Hashanah, Christmas, etc.) serve as control because:
- Fixed calendar timing (not controllable)
- Similar cultural significance
- Expected baseline clustering from legislative/business calendar effects

## Counter-Evidence & Limitations

### Specificity Test

Not all religious events correlate with policy shifts:

| Event Type | Correlation Rate | Classification |
|------------|------------------|----------------|
| Routine Temple Mount activities | Baseline | Noise |
| Annual holidays | 19.9% | Chance |
| Rare ceremonies (Red Heifer) | **50.7%** | Signal |

This specificity strengthens the hypothesis: only **rare, controllable** ritual events show anomalous clustering.

### Alternative Explanations

| Explanation | Assessment |
|-------------|------------|
| Fiscal year-end effects | Partially explains December; doesn't explain July/other clusters |
| Legislative calendar | Contributes to baseline; doesn't explain 3.5x ratio |
| Selection bias | Possible; mitigated by using pre-defined event lists |
| Coincidence | p = 0.002 makes pure coincidence unlikely |

### Data Limitations

1. **Sample size:** 73 ritual events, 121 holidays (moderate)
2. **December 2025 weighting:** Single cluster may skew results
3. **Gulf SWF positioning:** Q4 2025 data awaits 13F disclosure
4. **Causation:** Correlation documented, mechanism inferred
