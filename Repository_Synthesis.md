*How DOGE_Global_Effects, BRICS-NDB-LocalCurrency-DiD, and Epstein_Files_Uses_Theory form a unified analytical framework*

---

## Overview

These three repositories, developed over approximately three weeks, document different facets of the same structural shift in global power dynamics. Each uses rigorous statistical methodology to track correlations at different timescales and from different vantage points.

| Repository | Focus | Primary Metric | Lag Pattern |
|------------|-------|----------------|-------------|
| **Epstein_Files_Uses_Theory** | Document releases → compliance events | r = 0.6196 | 14-day lag |
| **DOGE_Global_Effects** | USAID cuts → instability → adversary backfill | r = 0.42-0.69 | 3-6 month (cuts→uprising), 6-12 month (uprising→backfill) |
| **BRICS-NDB-LocalCurrency-DiD** | BRICS expansion → local currency lending | +25.5 pp (p < 0.001) | Treatment effect post-2024 Kazan |

**The unified thesis**: US domestic institutional chaos creates foreign policy vacuums that adversaries systematically capture, while alternative financial architecture absorbs departing states.

## The Three-Layer Model

### Layer 1: Attention Capture (Epstein_Files_Uses_Theory)

**What it tracks**: How high-visibility domestic events ("flashbangs") correlate with quieter institutional movements.

**Core mechanism**: The 14-day lagged friction-compliance correlation suggests that attention cycles have predictable durations. When domestic controversy peaks, institutional actors have windows to execute changes with reduced scrutiny.

**Key data points**:
- Document release timing correlates with compliance events (r = 0.6196, p < 0.05)
- "Flashbang" events (cabinet controversies, file releases, scandals) consume media bandwidth
- The "Main Characters" (Patel, Hegseth, Noem, Bongino) generate predictable saturation patterns

**Role in synthesis**: This layer explains *why* the other patterns can occur without generating proportional public attention.

---

### Layer 2: Vacuum Creation (DOGE_Global_Effects)

**What it tracks**: How USAID funding cuts correlate with political instability in recipient countries.

**Core mechanism**: The 83% average USAID budget reduction ($24B terminated) creates governance and stability vacuums that manifest as uprisings 3-6 months later.

**Key data points**:
- N=20 countries analyzed (8 historical uprisings, 12 projections)
- Correlation: r = 0.419 full dataset (p = 0.066), r = 0.690 excluding Bangladesh outlier
- Adversary backfill follows 6-12 months post-uprising
- China BRI + Russia PMC deployments systematically target affected regions

**Geographic overlap with Epstein_Files_Uses_Theory Implications.md**:

| Country | DOGE Status | Implications.md Status |
|---------|-------------|------------------------|
| Nigeria | Historical uprising; $600M health cuts | $20B Ogidigben gas park; dedicated China partnership office |
| South Africa | PEPFAR terminations | First-ever Chinese HIV funding (Nov 2025) |
| Kenya | Finance bill protests (39 deaths) | China demining grant (immediate response) |
| Ethiopia | BRICS new partner | FOCAC beneficiary |
| Egypt | 84% non-military cuts | BRICS partner country |

**Role in synthesis**: This layer documents the *supply side* of vacuum creation—how US retrenchment generates instability that requires alternative patrons.

---

### Layer 3: Alternative Architecture (BRICS-NDB-LocalCurrency-DiD)

**What it tracks**: How BRICS expansion correlates with increased local-currency (non-USD) lending through the New Development Bank.

**Core mechanism**: The 2024 Kazan summit expanded BRICS from 5 founders to 13 members, then to 22 with the 2025 Rio partner tier. This expansion correlates with a 25.5 percentage point increase in NDB projects financed in local currency vs USD.

**Key data points**:
- 3,275 NDB approved projects (2015–Nov 2025)
- Treatment effect: +25.5 pp local currency share (p < 0.001, 10k permutation test)
- Parallel trends verified pre-2024
- Control group: MINT countries (Mexico, Indonesia, Nigeria, Turkey)

**Role in synthesis**: This layer documents the *demand side*—the financial infrastructure absorbing countries moving away from US-dollar dependency.

## Methodological Consistency

All three repositories use similar analytical approaches:

| Element | Epstein Theory | DOGE | BRICS-NDB |
|---------|----------------|------|-----------|
| **Primary statistic** | Pearson r | Pearson r | Difference-in-differences |
| **Significance testing** | p-value | p-value | Permutation test (10k) |
| **Lag analysis** | 14-day | 3-6 month, 6-12 month | Pre/post treatment |
| **Outlier treatment** | Documented | Sensitivity analysis (Bangladesh) | Robustness checks |
| **Limitations stated** | Yes | Extensive section | Yes |
| **Causal claims** | Avoided | Avoided | Avoided |
| **Data sources** | Public + leaked | Public + leaked (CGD) | NDB official + SWIFT |

**Shared epistemology**: All three repos explicitly state "correlation ≠ causation" and provide extensive limitations sections. The framework is *hypothesis-generating*, not confirmatory.
